jesus parables of the kingdom (παραβολή)

the sower, “the watershed of the parables”

  1. Matthew 13:1-23, Mark 4:1-20, Luke 8:4-15
  2. three-step presentation
    1. the parable itself
    2. disciples ask Jesus a question
    3. Jesus allegorical interpretation
  3. whom do we usually identify as the sower?
    1. Word of God, Johannine teaching — God himself
    2. the Word has to mean the eternal Son
    3. Jesus has already, and literally, been sown everywhere in the world — and quite without a single bit of earthly cooperation or even consent
    4. Jesus interpretation makes the parable profoundly complex
  4. Jesus explanation
    1. catholicity — it is at work everywhere, always, and for all
      1. “the field is the world”
      2. gentiles might be brought in; worse yet, no guarantee that the chosen people might not be left out
    2. mystery — left-handed rather than right-handed uses of power
      1. seed and sowing (Matt 13:24-27, Matt 13:31, Matt 17:20, John 12:24)
      2. imagery of seed — disproportionately small
      3. seeds disappear
      4. Jesus entire work proceeds as does the work of a seed; it takes place in a mystery, in secret — in a way that, as Luther said, can neither be known nor felt, but only believed, trusted
    3. present actuality — seeds do work
      1. seed that falls on the road and is eaten by birds
        1. just as the birds recognize seed for what it is even if the pavement doesn't, so the devil recognizes the power of the Word even when human beings don't
        2. the Word, like the seed, still works on its own terms
      2. seed that falls into the other three situations — the seed actually does its proper, reproductive work
    4. hostility and response
      1. Christians have denied the reality of praeter-human evil, or they have given the Old Deceiver far more time and attention than deserved
        1. hosts of evil, in the mystery of his death and resurrection, beaten by Jesus
        2. the devil has no power against the Word
      2. seed eaten by birds is as much seed as the seed that produced a hundredfold — the snatching of the Word by the devil — and the rejection of it by the shallow and the choking of it by the worldly — all take place within the working of the kingdom, not prior to it or outside of it
      3. in our day and age, we have come to understand that seeds don't do all the work — but in Jesus' day and for a very long time after it, that was not the common suppostion
      4. karpos (fruit)
        1. “true vine” (John 15), “works of the flesh and the fruits of the Spirit” (Galations 5:16-26)
        2. call for a response from us; but that response is to be one that is appropriate not to the accomplishing of a work but the bearing of fruit
